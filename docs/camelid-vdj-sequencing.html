01Camelid Germline VDJ Gene Dataset

IMGT reference directory for camelid immunoglobulin heavy chain germline genes. All camelid IGHV genes belong to the IGHV3 subgroup (closest to human VH3).

Two Distinct IGHV Gene Sets in Camelids
Set 1 (Conventional IgG1): IGHV3S1-S41 — expressed in conventional tetrameric IgG1 with light chains.
Set 2 (VHH / Heavy-Chain-Only): IGHV3S42-S74 — expressed in gamma-2 and gamma-3 chains lacking CH1 domain. These carry the hallmark hydrophilic substitutions at FR2 positions 42, 49, 50, 52 (IMGT numbering), enabling VHH to function without a light chain.

Naive Library Construction

Naive (Non-immunized) Libraries
Naive libraries are constructed from non-immunized animals using PBMCs and/or spleens. A landmark mega-diverse library used whole blood from 75 camelids (51 alpacas + 13 llamas + 11 Bactrian camels) plus spleens from 2 Bactrian camels, achieving high diversity without target-specific immunization.

Implications for VHH Sequencing

Illumina
Best for: High-throughput repertoire profiling
Highest accuracy for SNV/SHM detection. Cannot cover full VHH (~360 nt) in single read with MiSeq 2x250bp without paired-end overlap assembly. Dominant platform for CDR3 profiling.
PacBio HiFi
Best for: Full-length VDJ with high accuracy
Full VHH + constant region in single HiFi read with >99.9% accuracy. Ideal for paired VDJ + isotype analysis. FLAIRR-seq workflow enables full immunoglobulin repertoire. Higher cost per sample.
Nanopore
Best for: Rapid, portable full-length sequencing
Full-length VDJ in single read. FLIRseq workflow validated for B/T cell receptor sequencing. Real-time analysis enables adaptive sampling. Homopolymer errors may affect CDR calling accuracy. Improving rapidly with R10.4.1+.

China IP Landscape

China's Strategic Position
China has seen rapid growth in nanopore sequencing patent filings, driven by government backing in biotechnology self-sufficiency. Qitan Technology holds ~30 global patents covering pore proteins, membrane chemistry, and hardware design. BGI/MGI has independently developed CycloneSEQ with proprietary pore engineering, chip design, and basecalling algorithms developed over a decade of R&D. This positions China as the only country besides the UK (Oxford Nanopore) with fully domestic commercial nanopore sequencing capabilities.
